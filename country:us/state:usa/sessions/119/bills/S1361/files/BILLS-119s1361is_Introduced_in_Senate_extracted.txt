Title: 
Official Title: To require the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to give priority in the consideration of suicide prevention grants to entities located in States that have not received such a grant, and for other purposes.
Number of Sections: 2
Source: versions - Introduced in Senate
Media Type: text/xml

================================================================================

Section 1:
1.Short titleThis Act may be cited as the Every State Counts for Veterans Mental Health Act.

Section 2:
2.Priority for Department of Veterans Affairs suicide prevention grant recipients in States in which a grant has not been awardedSection 201(d) of the Commander John Scott Hannon Veterans Mental Health Care Improvement Act of 2019 (Public Law 116–171; 10 U.S.C. 1720F note) is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:(3)Additional priority for States that have not received a grant(A)In generalThe Secretary shall prioritizing consideration of any eligible entity located in a State in which an entity has applied but not received a grant under this section.(B)Scoring preferenceIf no entity in a particular State has received a grant under this section, the Secretary shall give all eligible entities in that State that apply for such a grant a scoring preference until at least one grant is awarded to an eligible entity in that State..
